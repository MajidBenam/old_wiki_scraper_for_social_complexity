Law

♠ Formal legal code ♣ inferred present ♥ Not discovered.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]Few codes known to exist. However, the law system was nationally integrated such that local disputes could be appealed to a higher court.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]Legal Text of Mose concerns control of land tenure and tax obligations. "In a case that went through five lawsuits over control of, or rights to, disputed land in the Village of Neshi, the plaintive Mose, as descendant of Neshi sought to overturn the judgement of of the Qenbet, "council" or court of magistrates in Memphis." This was the local court that had jurisdiction over the case "at the level of the nome, because Memphis was the capital of the nome in which the disputed land was located." The case moved up to the "Great Qenbet over which the Vizier presided, at national level." [MAJIDBENAM_REF_122_EgNKThu][MAJIDBENAM_BR]
Inscriptions record pharaonic decrees on crimes and punishments. Instructions for Merikare, written in Middle Kingdom, "set down basic guidelines for administering justice" and was well known in the New Kingdom.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_123_EgNKThu][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Legal system based on precedent and case law. [MAJIDBENAM_REF_124_EgNKThu][MAJIDBENAM_BR]

Specialized Buildings: polity owned

♠ irrigation systems ♣ present ♥ Irrigation was a local responsibility throughout the Pharaonic period. Central authority primarily concerned with taxation. However, central government was responsible for national projects like land reclamation and irrigation of new areas in Fayyum and Delta. Techniques employed included extension of canal network for the control of flood waters, and the human powered shaduf system. [MAJIDBENAM_REF_136_EgNKThu] Shaduf introduced middle second millennium BCE [MAJIDBENAM_REF_137_EgNKThu], which would be around start of the New Kingdom.

Transport infrastructure

♠ Roads ♣ present ♥ Western coast road.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_148_EgNKThu] Royal Road at Amarna. [MAJIDBENAM_REF_149_EgNKThu] Reliable road through Sinai had to be developed in order to advance by land into Canaan. Connected east Delta (or Avaris, at Perunefer) to Gaza.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_150_EgNKThu] Road network emerged with development of irrigation systems. Excavated soil was piled by the side of ditches, these formed embankments which were used as paths and roads. Generally not paved. (An exception was the 11.5 km paved straight road - using flagstones and petrified wood - discovered in the Fayyum, which artefacts date to Old Kingdom).[MAJIDBENAM_REF_151_EgNKThu]
♠ Bridges ♣ present ♥ Bridges over wide expanse of water unknown. Small bridges were built. A bridge at Amarna linked two parts of a royal palace that was separated by a Royal Road. It was supported by two pillars 5 meters apart.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_152_EgNKThu]
♠ Canals ♣ present ♥ Thutmose I (r c1525-1512 BCE) re-excavated the canals. [MAJIDBENAM_REF_153_EgNKThu]
♠ Ports ♣ present ♥ Memphis and Aswan were two large ports. At Avaris there was a dockyard called Perunefer.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_154_EgNKThu]
