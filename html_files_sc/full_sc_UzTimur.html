"The most senior officers were granted the ultimate title of tarkhan, a position harking back to the days of Genghis Khan. This conferred on them a number of important privileges, among which the most valuable was the permanent exemption from taxes. Unlike any other soldier in Temur's armies, the tarkhan was entitled to keep everything he plundered. Everyone else had to make over a share of the spoils to the emperor.
