Administrative officials
The long list of administrative officials includes: diviras (clerks) and akshapatalikas (revenue officers) [MAJIDBENAM_REF_29_InChaBd]; baladhirkta or mahabaladhirtkas (military officials with administrative duties); dutakas (in charge of conveyance of royal grants); durgapatis (fort administrators); dandapasika and chauradhikarana (in charge of crime and punishment); chatas and batas (possibly police-like officers); vasavakas (in charge of arranging the residences of touring officials and foreigners); viniyuktakas (unclear); gamagamikas (supervised egress and ingress of travellers, including inspecting "passports") [MAJIDBENAM_REF_30_InChaBd][MAJIDBENAM_BR]5.

Defeated rulers whom the Emperor trusted to keep in charge of their territories, now made into vishayas, or provinces [MAJIDBENAM_REF_32_InChaBd]. It is not entirely clear, from the source, whether rajasamanta and "governor" are the same office.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]3. Samantas
Feudal subordinates of the rajasamantas, they provided troops and tribute to the Emperor when required [MAJIDBENAM_REF_33_InChaBd].[MAJIDBENAM_BR]4. Town assemblies
Made up of elders (mahajanas), guild chiefs, mahallakas, and "head of business communities" [MAJIDBENAM_REF_34_InChaBd].[MAJIDBENAM_BR]5. Village administration
Made up of mahajanas (elders), mahattaras, mahattaradhikarins and gavundas (royal representatives) [MAJIDBENAM_REF_35_InChaBd]. There also existed "gramabhogikas" or "village leaders" and karanas or "village accountants"[MAJIDBENAM_REF_36_InChaBd], but it is unclear what their position was in relation to other village administrators.

Law

♠ Formal legal code ♣ present ♥ Smirtis and dharmashastras [MAJIDBENAM_REF_57_InChaBd]. The Smriti, or Manu-smriti, is a collection of texts prescribing correct behaviour, including a section explicitly devoted to "the law of kings" [MAJIDBENAM_REF_58_InChaBd], while the dharmashastras are a collection of more explicitly legal texts [MAJIDBENAM_REF_59_InChaBd].
♠ Judges ♣ present ♥ The Emperor was the supreme judge, but he also dispensed justice through judges he himself appointed [MAJIDBENAM_REF_60_InChaBd].
