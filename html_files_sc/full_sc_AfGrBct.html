[MAJIDBENAM_REF_61_AfGrBct] The Bactrian Greeks maintained an uncorrupted greek written language tradition. To date, a Greek inscription has been found in a cave at Qara Kamar in northern Bactria, on the frontier of modern Turkmenistan and Uzbekistan. Money

♠ Articles ♣ present ♥ The currency of the Bactrian Greeks is one of the only means of providing a chronological history of the polity. Although initially issuing coinage under the auspices of the authority of the Seleucid King, rulers in Bactria quickly issued their own gold coins from the mint in Ai Khanoum. The Bactrian Kings also minted silver, bronze and nickel coins. There have also been limited discoveries of in the northern steppes that have been used to establish the boundaries of the polity by some scholars. [MAJIDBENAM_REF_73_AfGrBct]
