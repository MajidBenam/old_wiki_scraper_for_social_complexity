Law

♠ Formal legal code ♣ inferred present ♥ Note: This is the code for Abbasid Caliphate. [MAJIDBENAM_REF_56_EgTulIk][MAJIDBENAM_BR]In the Abbasid Caliphate formal the law was promulgated by a body known as the Fuqaha. The law code was heavily influenced by Sharia law. Sharia was based on the Sunna, which were teachings of the Prophet Muhammad, and the Quran, the holy book of Islam. Legal thought was also influenced by Ijma’, which were a body of rulings on legal issues based on the consensus of scholars who had met to discuss specific cases. Despite the Caliphate’s claims to religious authority based on their links to the Prophet Muhammed, it was rare for direct rulings on legal matters to originate from the caliphal authorities. Alongside a developing legal code was the development of the Qudis, who were full time judiciary officials.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_57_EgTulIk]
