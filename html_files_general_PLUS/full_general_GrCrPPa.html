Kydonia (Chania), in west Crete, seems to have been a centre of significant importance during Late Minoan IIIB (1300-1200 BCE). Tablets written in Linear B script and inscribed locally produced stirrup jars used to transport aromatic oils points to a central administration. [MAJIDBENAM_REF_3_GrCrPPa] There is no evidence, however, to argue that Kydonia was the seat of a central political authority controlling Crete.
