However, on other maps [6] it is not highlighted.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Western Zhou Dynasty was the first Chinese state to claim the Mandate of Heaven, the divinely bestowed right to rule. Zhou was a tributary state to Shang until the Zhou king Zhou Wu Wang defeated the last king of Shang in the 1046 BCE Battle of Muye.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_17_CnWZhou] Zhou power was consolidated after the Duke of Zhou's defeat of the Rebellion of the Three Guards, led by Shang loyalists and separatist eastern nobles.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_18_CnWZhou] In defeating the rebellion, the Zhou state was able to add a large area of land in eastern China to its territory.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_19_CnWZhou][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Western Zhou established their capital at Haojing, and the Duke of Zhou later established Chengzhou as a second capital.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_20_CnWZhou] In 957 BCE, the Zhou controlled territory covering an estimated 850,000 square kilometres based in the central plains of China.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]The period was peaceful for the first 75 years of Zhou rule.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_21_CnWZhou] However, the decentralization of Zhou power into fiefdoms encouraged turmoil between states, popular unrest, and vassal rebellions.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_22_CnWZhou] The Marquess of Shen sacked Haojing and killed the 12th Zhou king over a succession dispute in 771 BCE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_23_CnWZhou] The crown prince subsequently moved the capital to Luoyang and founded the Eastern Zhou dynasty.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Western Zhou are noted for their introduction of the Mandate of Heaven, their kinship-based feudal system and their use of lineage law.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_24_CnWZhou] The state's kinship-based feudal system encouraged the spread of Zhou writing, culture and identity.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_25_CnWZhou] Some scholars have seen Zhou lineage law, with its emphasis on 'lineage rituals, familial ethics, and beneficent rule', as an intellectual precursor of Confucianism.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_26_CnWZhou] The hierarchies, division of labour and meritocratic practices that emerged under the Western Zhou also helped lay the foundations for the introduction of bureaucracy.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_27_CnWZhou][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Population and political organization[MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Western Zhou state was a proto-feudal monarchy in which feudal lords were supported by an extended family network.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_28_CnWZhou] The first king of Zhou introduced the fengjian system, which made military leaders and members of the royal family into regional lords ruling over parcels of land.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_29_CnWZhou] These fiefdoms were then divided into smaller units and distributed to members of the local rulers' families.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_30_CnWZhou][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Individual fiefdoms had their own taxes, legal systems, and currencies but
