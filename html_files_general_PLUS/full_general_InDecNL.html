♠ Linguistic family ♣ Dravidian ♥ [MAJIDBENAM_REF_5_InDecNL][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The South Indian Neolithic lasted from about 3000 to 1200 BCE. Here we are particularly interested in the northern part of the modern-day Indian state of Karnataka, where Neolithic communities appear to have been small, egalitarian, and reliant on pastoralism (mostly cattle), agriculture (mostly millet and pulses), and hunting and gathering. The prevalence of cattle motifs in rock art, as well as the number of ashmounds (large mounds of burned cattle dung) dotting the landscape, point to the symbolic importance of cattle in South Indian Neolithic ideology as a whole.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_6_InDecNL][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Population and political organization[MAJIDBENAM_BR]The presence of only minor variations in house size, design and content, as well as in mortuary practices, suggests an egalitarian society during this period.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_7_InDecNL] No population estimates are provided by the literature.
