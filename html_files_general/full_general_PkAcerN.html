Map showing key archaeological sites in the Kachi Plain.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Kachi Plain, in modern-day Pakistan, is hemmed in on two of its three sides by the mountains of Baluchistan, while its southeastern side opens up to the Indus Valley.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_6_PkAcerN] The earliest evidence for agriculture here was found in Mehrgarh and dates to 7000 BCE. It is impossible to say whether Mehrgarh was part of a wider network of agricultural communities in the region, or whether it was unique and/or isolated. Besides agriculture, the inhabitants of Mehrgarh also relied, at this time, on hunting and gathering, but not yet on pastoralism.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_7_PkAcerN][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Population and political organization[MAJIDBENAM_BR]It is not possible to give an accurate estimate of the region's population at this time,[MAJIDBENAM_REF_8_PkAcerN] and the size of occupied Mehrgarh is uncertain, as the population shifted over time and part of the site has been cut away by the Bolan River.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_9_PkAcerN] Similarly, the literature does not provide many clues as to the political organization of Mehrgarh or any other site in the region at this time.
