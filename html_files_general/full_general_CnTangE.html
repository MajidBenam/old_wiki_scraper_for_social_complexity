♠ Alternate Religion#2 ♣ ♥"Map 11. T'ang China 742 CE." [MAJIDBENAM_REF_12_CnTangE][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Tang Dynasty is widely considered a cultural and political high point of imperial China. The dynasty was founded by Li Yuan, the Duke of Tang, when the threat of insurrection forced the previous Sui dynasty court to flee from Luoyang, the capital, to Yangzhou. Li Yuan marched to Luoyang and seized the abandoned capital in 618 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_13_CnTangE] He became the first emperor of the Tang dynasty (r. 618-626 CE) and is posthumously known as Gaozu. Under the Early Tang Dynasty, the capital was moved from Chang'an to Luoyang.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_14_CnTangE] We divide the Dynasty into an Early period (618-763 CE) and Late period (763-907 CE), separated by the decline in imperial authority and instability of experienced by the Tang in the 750s, culminating in the An Lushan rebellion to close out the Early period (755‒763 CE).[MAJIDBENAM_BR]Under Early Tang leadership, China's territory expanded considerably. Conquered territories included large areas of Central Asia and northern Korea (Koguryō).[MAJIDBENAM_REF_15_CnTangE] In later years, however, China pursued a defensive, non-expansionist policy towards groups on the steppe and frontier.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_16_CnTangE][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The dynasty, also known as the Tang (T'ang) Empire or Li Dynasty, is famous for its poetry, literature, increased trade and general cosmopolitanism.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_17_CnTangE] In 660 CE, Empress Wu became the first woman to rule China, first governing as a regent to her young son and later ruling as empress dowager and regent until her death in 705 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_18_CnTangE] Emperor Xuanzong's 44-year reign (712‒756 CE) ushered in a cultural and economic golden age, which declined as he aged and ended in rebellion and an overthrow of the dynasty.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_19_CnTangE][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Population and political organization[MAJIDBENAM_BR]Emperor Gaozu worked to restore control of the imperial government that had been established by the Sui Dynasty, and founded frontier garrisons controlled directly by the capital.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_20_CnTangE] The Tang instituted the much discussed 'equal fields' system, in which land owned by the state was parcelled out in equal allotments to citizens in return for taxation. The Tang also minted many new coins in an attempt to stabilize the economy.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_21_CnTangE][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Early Tang imperial government was characterized by an emperor who theoretically had absolute power, but was often in practice overruled by ministers or regents.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_22_CnTangE] The central government was headed by three chief ministers who ran the Imperial Chancellery, Imperial Secretariat, and the Department for State Affairs.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_23_CnTangE] The government also included a large central and state bureaucracy, marked by the expanding use of merit examinations.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_24_CnTangE][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The population of the Early Tang Dynasty is estimated at 37 million in 700 CE and increased to almost 53 million by 754 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_25_CnTangE] In the 8th century, there were an estimated 1 million people living in Chang'an.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_26_CnTangE]
