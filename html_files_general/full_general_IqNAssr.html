♠ Alternate Religion ♣ ♥The Assyrian Empire (911-612 BCE) was a powerful polity that expanded from its heartland in northern Iraq, using the most advanced military technologies of the era: two wheeled chariots, cavalry and an infantry fully converted to iron weapons.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_12_IqNAssr][MAJIDBENAM_REF_13_IqNAssr][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The most important early ruler was Assurnasirpall II (883-859 BCE) who built the capital Kalhu on the east bank of the Tigris.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_14_IqNAssr] The Assyrian king maintained his presence by establishing “royal cities” with palaces throughout the realm, which he appears to have used on a regular basis.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_15_IqNAssr] The Assyrian capital also changed frequently: the first capital was Ashur (911-859 BCE) and the last was at Ninevah (681-612 BCE). Sharrukin was important between 707-705 BCE.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]Despite the constantly moving king and capital the Assyrian government put down literary roots in a state archives[MAJIDBENAM_REF_16_IqNAssr] and perhaps the world’s first organized library was built at Nineveh under Asurbanipal (668-627 BCE). Here scribes copied texts from a 1,000 year old Babylonian literary tradition.
