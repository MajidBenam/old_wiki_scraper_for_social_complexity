♠ Religion ♣ ♥China’s Han dynasty is divided into two periods: Western Han or Former Han (202 BCE-9 CE), and Eastern Han or Later Han (25-220 CE). The period between the two Han dynasties was an interregnum ruled by Wang Mang who overthrow the Han and founded the short-lived Xin dynasty.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_13_CnEHan*] Rulers of the Western and Eastern Han are descended from Han founder Liu Bang.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_14_CnEHan*] The Han dynasty was reinstated when military troops revolted against Wang Mang and attacked the capital of Chang’an in 23 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_15_CnEHan*] The first recognized Eastern Han emperor Emperor Guangwudi moved the capital to Luoyang in 25 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_16_CnEHan*][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Buddhism spread into China during the Eastern Han period. The religion soon began to influence Chinese morals and ethics.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_17_CnEHan*] Han innovation continued into the Eastern Han period. The eunuch Cai Lun invented paper made from mulberry bark in 105 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_18_CnEHan*][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The decline of the Eastern Han was marked by series of natural disasters including floods and plagues beginning in 168 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_19_CnEHan*] The disasters were accompanied by two large peasant uprisings: the Yellow Turban Rebellion and the Five Pecks of Rice rebellion.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_20_CnEHan*] The Eastern Han also faced constant rebellions from Qiang ethnic minorities.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_21_CnEHan*] Provincial warlords aided the central government in suppressing these major rebellions. These warlords eventually became rulers of the provinces.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_22_CnEHan*] Warlord Dong Zhou seized Luoyang in 190 CE but was defeated by the warlord Cao Cao.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_23_CnEHan*] Eastern Han emperors stayed on the throne but the Han empire was split between three generals, ushering in the Three Kingdoms period.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_24_CnEHan*][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Eastern Han territory covered 6.5 million square kilometers in 100 CE, but only 2.5 million square kilometers by 200 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_25_CnEHan*] At its peak, the Han dynasty encompassed modern China, northern Vietnam, Inner Mongolia, southern Manchuria, and parts of modern Korea.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_26_CnEHan*][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Population and political organization[MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Eastern Han continued many of the administrative practices of the Western Han.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_27_CnEHan*] However, the dynasty was marked by bloody political infighting including succession conflicts, and attempts to grab power by consort clans and eunuch cliques.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_28_CnEHan*] Eunuchs had a strong influence in the Eastern Han government and competed with Confucian officials and the imperial clan. [MAJIDBENAM_REF_29_CnEHan*] The decline of the Eastern Han was marked by the rise of strong provincial rulers with independent armies, or warlords, and a weakening of the corrupt central government.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]The population of the Eastern Han was between 48 and 50 million people in 140 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_30_CnEHan*]. Luoyang was home to 420,000 people in 100 CE, but only 100,000 by 200 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_31_CnEHan*][MAJIDBENAM_REF_32_CnEHan*]
