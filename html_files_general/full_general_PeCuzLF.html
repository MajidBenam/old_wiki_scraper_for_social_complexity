♠ Religion Sect ♣ suspected unknown ♥The Formative period in the Cuzco valley (2200-500 BCE) marks the transition from small-scale semi-nomadic groups of hunter-gatherers to sedentary villages associated with ceramic production and agriculture. Traditionally, it has been subdivided into three periods. The Early Formative (2200-1500 BCE) corresponds to the beginning of ceramic production and quinoa cultivation and the establishment of large, permanent villages.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_17_PeCuzLF] During the Middle Formative (1500-500 BCE), Marcavalle ceramics appeared and villages grew, possibly leading to the beginnings of ranked village societies.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_18_PeCuzLF] The domestication of camelids was also under way.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_19_PeCuzLF] The Late Formative (500 BCE-200 CE) saw the emergence of a three-tiered settlement pattern in the Cuzco and Oropesa basins, dominated by the settlement of Wimpillay.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_20_PeCuzLF] This period is also known as Chanapata, in reference to a dominant ceramic style discovered in the 1940s.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_21_PeCuzLF] It is possible that other small chiefdoms existed in the region: a few early villages have been found near Raqchi in the Chit'apampa Basin,[MAJIDBENAM_REF_22_PeCuzLF] and there may have been some small independent polities near Paruro and Cusichaca.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_23_PeCuzLF] In the Lucre Basin to the east of modern Cuzco, a small chiefdom may have centred around the site of Choquepukio.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_24_PeCuzLF][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Population and political organization[MAJIDBENAM_BR]The population of these early polities remains unknown, but over 80 archaeological sites dating to this period have been surveyed in the valley by archaeologist Brian Bauer.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_25_PeCuzLF] Some of these were identified as hamlets and small villages, with between a few dozen and a few hundred inhabitants.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_26_PeCuzLF] More research is needed in order to understand sociopolitical relations at the time, but Bauer has interpreted the Late Formative as the period in which chiefdoms begin to emerge.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_27_PeCuzLF] This process continued and solidified in the Early Intermediate Period.
