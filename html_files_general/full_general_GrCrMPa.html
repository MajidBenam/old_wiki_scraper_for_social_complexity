This period began following the destruction of many Minoan sites around 1450, due either to natural catastrophes or human agency[MAJIDBENAM_REF_6_GrCrMPa][MAJIDBENAM_REF_7_GrCrMPa], and it ended with the destruction of Knossos[MAJIDBENAM_REF_8_GrCrMPa] Throughout this period, Knossos was the main political, administrative and economic centre of the island: analyses of both textual and archaeological data shows that Knossos controlled a series of second-order (e.g. Kydonia and Phaistos) and third-order (e.g. Tylissos) centers[MAJIDBENAM_REF_9_GrCrMPa][MAJIDBENAM_REF_10_GrCrMPa].
