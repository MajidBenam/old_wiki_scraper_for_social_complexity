♠ Religion Genus ♣ Islam ♥ "Simultaneously, in some of the states that arose in the wake of the Mongol invasions, political authority and state organization were frankly derived from the nomadic traditions and customs referred to as the Great Yasa of Changiz Khan. With the definitive conversion of the Mongols in Iran to Islam at the beginning of the fourteenth/eighth century, however, a curious amalgam of legitimizing principles evolved, in which the Sacred Law of a decapitated Sunni-Jama'i Islamic universal state became inextricably bound up with the concepts and ideals of the devolving nomadic Changizkhanid world empire." [MAJIDBENAM_REF_15_IrAkKoy] "After 1467/872, the Aqquyunlu rulers Uzun Hasan and his son Ya'qub endeavored to transform the polity into a traditional Irano-Islamic agrarian empire by gaining physical control of the land and its inhabitants, as well as the centers of commercial activity, by reforming administrative practice to extract maximum economic advantage, by developing a truly sumbiotic relationship with the sedentary populations under their rule, and by substituting universalizing Islamic legitimizing principles and institutions for particularistic nomadic ideals, kinship ties, and personal loyalties." [MAJIDBENAM_REF_16_IrAkKoy] A few examples of Uzun Hasan's deeds as 'renewer' of Islamic Faith: "In return for such recognition by the high Islamic religious establishment, Uzun Hasan made a clear commitment to strengthen that institution and to patronize its members. Writing to Prince Bayezid b. Fatih Mehmed in September 1470/Rabi' II 875, for example, Uzun Hasan boasts that his entire administration has been set in accordance with the principles of the Sacred Law and secular justice. Abominable practices rife under the Qaraquyunlu such as fornication, sodomy, prostitution, wining, gambling, and the desecration of religious buildings have been utterly abolished, and extreme antinomian dervish groups such as the Qalandars and the Haydaris have been suppressed. He further claims to have regulated pious foundations and endowments, reinstated religious personnel in the mosques, and encouraged religious college enrollments. Mosques in major centers such as Amid and Isfahan, as well as in smaller towns, were repaired with funds from Uzun Hasan's personal, legitimately acquired fortune (mal-i halal), and commemorative inscriptions were executed to afford him yet another medium in which to express his support of the Sacred Law and to assert his claims on Muslim loyalties." [MAJIDBENAM_REF_17_IrAkKoy] "In addition to fostering and supporting aristocratic Islamic culture, Ya'qub's government was also concerned with manipulating Islamic symbols of sovereignty in a renewed effort to buttress the politico-religious legitimacy of the regime." ♠ Alternate Religion ♣ ♥[MAJIDBENAM_REF_23_IrAkKoy][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Ak Koyunlu were a loose confederation of nomadic Turkman tribes that ruled in Iran between 1339-1501 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_24_IrAkKoy] They formed an alliance with the Timurid Emirate (1370-1526 CE) until Uzun Hasan (r. c1453-1478 CE) declared himself an independent sultan.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_25_IrAkKoy] Their rule was ended by the Safavids in 1501 CE.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_26_IrAkKoy][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The initial Ak Koyunlu government system was not complex; the sultan, a member of the Bayandor clan, was the head of a confederation[MAJIDBENAM_REF_27_IrAkKoy] and obliged to attend and abide by the decisions of a powerful council of Amirs (kengac) and tribal chiefs (boy kanlari). This collective "determined military matters and the recurrent issue of succession to the sultanate".[MAJIDBENAM_REF_28_IrAkKoy] However, by Qara Otman (c1398 CE) the Ak Koyunlu had gained "at least a rudimentary bureaucratic apparatus of the Iranian-Islamic type."[MAJIDBENAM_REF_29_IrAkKoy] in addition to support from more tribes, and better relations with Christian sedentary people.[MAJIDBENAM_BR]In the second half of the fifthteenth century, the complexity of state institutions increased another step with the conquest of eastern Iran.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_30_IrAkKoy] Uzun Hasan maintained the existing administrative system as well as their officials.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_31_IrAkKoy] Woods (1998) notes that there is evidence of an attempt to standardize and regularize administrative and financial procedures.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_32_IrAkKoy]
