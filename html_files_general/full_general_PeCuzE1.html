[MAJIDBENAM_REF_22_PeCuzE1][MAJIDBENAM_BR][MAJIDBENAM_REF_23_PeCuzE1][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The Early Intermediate Period of Andean history lasted from 400 BCE to 550 CE,[MAJIDBENAM_REF_24_PeCuzE1] and is known for the emergence of regional forms of political organization, such as the Moche in northern Peru (100-800 CE) and the Nazca in the Rio Grande de Nazca and Ica regions (100 BC-800 CE). In the Cuzco Valley, this period saw the development of numerous chiefdoms of varying sizes.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_25_PeCuzE1] One of these polities is known as Qotakalli (200-500 CE),[MAJIDBENAM_REF_26_PeCuzE1] and may have controlled an area of up to 1000 square kilometres.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_27_PeCuzE1][MAJIDBENAM_BR]The period also saw a change in settlement patterns. Wimpillay no longer dominated the valley, as several new large sites grew in the west of the basin, with a possible large settlement under the modern city of Cusco.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_28_PeCuzE1] New settlements grew along the lower valley slopes below 3500 metres above sea level, which archaeologist Brian Bauer interprets as evidence for population growth and a possible shift in the valley's economy towards maize production.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_29_PeCuzE1][MAJIDBENAM_BR]In the Lucre Basin further to the east, the Chanapata culture still flourished in the form of small farming villages until 600 CE: Chanapata ceramics were found in the lowest strata during excavations at the site of Choquepukio.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_30_PeCuzE1] These polities may have centred around the sites of Choquepukio and Mama Qolda.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_31_PeCuzE1] Furthermore, the presence of Pucara ceramics and early Tiwanaku-related wares indicate possible contacts between the Cuzco Valley polities and the Titicaca cultural sphere, perhaps through trade, but not through political assimilation.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_32_PeCuzE1][MAJIDBENAM_REF_33_PeCuzE1][MAJIDBENAM_BR]Population and political organization[MAJIDBENAM_BR]Although the population of the region during this period is currently impossible to determine, it is worth mentioning that 16 Qotakalli sites with an area of between 1 and 5 hectares have been surveyed, as well as 35 sites between 0.25 and 1 hectares,[MAJIDBENAM_REF_34_PeCuzE1] suggesting a possible two-tiered settlement pattern.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_35_PeCuzE1] The density of sites near modern Cuzco may indicate various groups of elite households interacting with each other within the Qotakalli chiefdom.[MAJIDBENAM_REF_36_PeCuzE1]
